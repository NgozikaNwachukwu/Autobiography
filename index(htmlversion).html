A SHORT AUTOBIOGRAPHY
OF
MISS NGOZIKA NWACHUKWU

Birth and Family

My Name is Ngozika Blessing Nwachukwu and I'm 18 years old. I was Born on the 7th of December, 2005 in the city of Aba, Abia, Nigeria.
I currently reside in Toronto and I go to Ontario Tech University and I am currently in second year. I live with my 3 sisters; Laura, Karen and Kim.
I have a fourth sister, Sharon and a brother, David. My parents and brother still live back home and I miss them every day.
I am the 5th out of 6 children and I grew up in a very loving home and I cherish my family deeply.

Childhood

I started school around when I was 2 years old. I went to pre-kindergarten in Aba, Then when we moved to Abuja, I went to Kindergarten and primary in Living Fountain International.
While I was there, I mostly won awards for best in mathematics and science. Throughout my Kindergarten and Primary Those were the subjects I was Naturally good at and had a genuine interest for. I did not like arts, music, or anything creative because I wasnâ€™t very good at them. However, I still managed to get good grades in those subjects.
I never really worried nor cared about my appearance at the time, Grades were always where I shined the most at anyway.
The first friend I ever made was Sharon. We were in the same Kindergarten class. One day whilst in the English lesson, she kept asking the teacher to go to the bathroom but she didn't let her.
She ended up peeing on the floor, the teacher screamed at her to clean it up and everyone laughed at her but me. She started to cry and I felt like crying when I saw her cry. That is when I realized how empathetic I was. I felt like everyone was laughing at me and I hated that.
After she cleaned it up, I went to give her a napkin from my lunch bag to clean her tears, Then we became best friends. I was so excited to have my first friend that I got distracted from school and got my first B. I genuinely thought it was the end of the world and cried about it but I learnt how to balance having a friend and being a good student.
I often wonder where Sharon was today. And of course before I left the school I made a whole bunch of new friends as well.

About Me

My favorite color is Green, and I love dogs. I had a dog named Dexter, I loved that dog more than anything. My dad got him for me when I was 7 and he sadly died of cancer back in 2020 and I miss him everyday. I love fried eggs. I could Live on them alone if I wasn't going to be judged.
I love swimming and playing badminton and have gold medals from both sports. I would say I am a bit of a perfectionist, because I believe I must be the absolute best in everything I do and "anything worth doing must be done very well" as my Mom always says.
I am very empathetic naturally -as i said above. Seeing an old person sitting alone can make me break down and I'm that one person that cries every time they watch finding Nemo. I also hate horror movies, I genuinely get scared.
Overall, I am a very loyal and loving person, and I am also very dedicated and driven.

Young Adolescence

I left primary school after 5th grade and went to secondary school. The name of The secondary institution I attended was Regina Pacis and this was a very pivotal moment of my life.
This was around the time I decided who I wanted to be in life and part of that reason was because of my father. I was old enough to realize how privileged I am, and how blessed I was to have a comfortable life.
That's when I knew I wanted to be an engineer- just like my Father is. It was an amazing idea and i was already good in sciences so I knew it will be a good path to follow so I can be able to give my present and future family a comfortable life just like my dad did.
I initially decided to do aerospace engineering and become an astronaut, but when I got to High school I chickened out when I found out you can get stuck in space, and what is one exactly supposed to do in a situation like that?
I then decided to pursue software engineering because I thought and still think its really cool although I wish it was easier because it gets really stressful. But if it were easy, everyone would do it.
Anyway, while in this school -which for context was an all girl's boarding school- ironically is what drove my passion for intersectional feminism. Due to a lot of bad experiences I had there, made me want to be a women's rights activist/overall human's rights activist as well in the future, but I want to pursue engineering first.
It was not all bad though, I met my current best friend there and made a lot of good friends and memories on the way, Although I genuinely do not recommend it for anyone.

What Life Means To Me

Life to me is surrounding yourself with the right people -that is people you trust with your life. That might seem too much for some people but I would never surround myself with anyone I do not trust or Love.
Life for me is also being authentically yourself and doing/being/surrounding yourself with what and who makes you happy. At the end of the day we can die at any time, of literally any cause, so its better that you try your best to live your life to the fullest. Although I know it is hard. I always think, if I am in a situation that I'm about to die can i say "I lived a happy and fulfilling life?"
Life for me is also fighting for what you believe in till the end. Even if anyone makes me feel like it doesn't matter or it shouldn't matter that will never stop me from fighting for what I believe is right and fighting for the ones I love. And to in conclusion life is about upholding what you believe/your standards and never letting anyone or anything come between that.

Overall

To conclude, I am young and still have a very long way to go in life. Though, I feel like I might know it all, I know I still have a lot to learn in basically every sector of life. Nevertheless, I am thankful for all the experiences that I have had in life because they have shaped me into who I am today, and I hope that I will continue to go through life being unapologetically me.
Thank you so much for reading!
